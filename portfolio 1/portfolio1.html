A DAY IN QUARANTINE

By Ivan Lee & Kaitlyn Lam



Confined to our homes with social and physical distancing being the new norm, it is to no surprise that feelings of loneliness and isolation are felt more strongly than ever. Working with my partner Ivan Lee, our project aims to showcase the duality of living alone and living at home during the 2020 pandemic by comparing and contrasting the more social aspects of living with family, to the more isolating feeling of being confined alone.

MY ROLES

PRE-PRODUCTION, VISUAL EFFECTS & AUDIO ADJUSTMENTS

PRE-PRODUCTION

My main contributions to the pre-production process included creating a visual effect and storyboarding. Having done a viusal effects activity in week 6 of the course, I was able to learn the new skill of creating split screens through the software program Premiere Pro. Knowing that my partner and I intended to include this effect in our film, gaining the ability to create the split screen effect was useful in helping us envision our concept more clearly. In addition to creating visual effects, I also drew out storyboards of envisioned shots. By clarifying specific details, such as field of view, camera angles, and camera movements of each shot, I created a guide that helped my partner and I understand the direction of our film.
Besides technical skills, I also participated in weekly meetings to discuss and refine the vision of the story with my partner.




VISUAL EFFECTS



The visual effects I explored during this project were the split screen effect and the walk-by transition. Since split screens can be used to depict multiple shots simultaneously, this effect fit perfectly with the projects goal of emphasizing the juxtaposition of living at home and living alone. Additionally, the walk-by transition created a fun and unique element that enhanced the playful tone of our video. Watch the video below to see the effects!








A) SPLIT SCREEN

To achieve the split screen effect on Premiere Pro, I applied a crop function to the selected footages. Once applied, keyframes were used to mark the positions, the left crop, and the right crop, of the footages at both the initial and the end of where the split screen were applied. By adjusting the keynotes and crops of the footage, I was able to create a simple split screen effect.





With split screens allowing side by side shots to be displayed simultaneously, the film is able to visually compare and contrast the similarities and differences of family life and solo life. This, in turn, could enhance viewerâ€™s understandings of the project, as the intentions of depicting duality in our film becomes clearer when shots are physically shown side by side to compare. While split screens are effective in portraying duality, they can also be used to tell the story more efficiently in a given time frame. Showing multiple footages at once allocated more time for the interviews, which were equally important in conveying the message of our story.





B) WALK-BY TRANSITION

The walk-by transition was created using inverted masks and a pen tool. Starting from where my partner began to walk across the screen, and continuing on frame by frame, I used a pen tool outlining his figure to create an inverted mask shape. As my partner walked off screen, the mask allowed video footage underneath to be revealed gradually; this created a smooth transition from one shot to the next. Adding the walk-by transition created a fun and unique element to our playful vlog.












AUDIO ADJUSTMENTS

During the editing process I was also in charge of adjusting sound volume and matching audio clips to interview video footages. This included adjusting the volumes of the interview by unlinking, cutting, and adjusting audio through the audio tab, as well as matching audio by unlinking and re-positioning audio files in realtion to video files. Audio adjustment was an important step in ensuring our video was cohesive, producing a quality video with quality sound.
